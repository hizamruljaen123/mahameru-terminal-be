# Banking Analysis Instructions

Apply these rules when analyzing Indonesian banking sector or individual bank stocks.

## 7 Mandatory Rules for Banking Stocks

### Rule 1: Credit Growth Analysis
- Loan growth > 15% YoY: Potentially aggressive, check NPL trajectory
- Loan growth < 5% YoY: Weak demand or strategic conservatism
- Check composition: Consumer vs corporate loan mix

### Rule 2: Asset Quality (NPL/NPF)
- NPL > 3%: Concerning, requires detailed breakdown
- NPL < 2%: Healthy, but verify if conservative provisioning
- Watch sequential trend: 3 consecutive months rise = red flag
- Check NPL by segment: productive, consumptive, microservices

### Rule 3: Net Interest Margin (NIM)
- NIM > 5%: Strong pricing power, but check forundraising cost
- NIM < 3%: Margin pressure, likely competitive or mass segment focus
- Compare to peer average: +50bps deviation needs explanation

### Rule 4: Fee Income Diversification
- Fee income > 25% of total revenue: Good diversification
- Fee income < 10%: Highly dependent on credit cycle
- Watch: transaction banking, wealth management, treasury income

### Rule 5: Capital Adequacy (CAR)
- CAR > 20%: Well capitalized, room for growth
- CAR < 15%: Constraint on loan growth
- Check: CET1 vs AT1 vs Tier 2 composition

### Rule 6: Efficiency Ratio (CIR)
- CIR < 45%: Efficient operations
- CIR > 60%: Need transformation or facing headwinds
- Check: opex breakdown, personnel vs technology vs other

### Rule 7: Liquidity & Funding
- LDR > 90%: Tight liquidity, dependence on wholesale funding
- CASA ratio > 70%: Stable, low-cost funding base
- Check: CD ratio, loan-to-deposit trend

## Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify Bank Category
```
Large Cap / Systemically Important: BBCA, BMRI, BBNI
Mid Cap / Growth Oriented: BBRI, BTPN, BBTN
Digital / New Banking: Jago, SeaBank, Blu
```

### Step 2: Apply Category-Specific Benchmarks
- Compare against appropriate peer group
- Large cap: stability & consistency
- Mid cap: growth & efficiency gains

### Step 3: Cross-Reference Macro Factors
- BI policy rate: directly impacts NIM
- Rupiah stability: impacts cost of funds
- GDP growth: drives credit demand
- Banking sector ROE target: ~15-17% healthy

## Red Flags for Banking Analysis

1. **Rapid loan growth + declining NIM** = Potentially predatory lending
2. **Rising NPL + declining provision** = Earnings manipulation risk
3. **CASA decline + LDR rise** = Funding pressure
4. **Fee income spike without clear driver** = One-time or unsustainable
5. **CAR decline + loan growth acceleration** = Capital erosion

## Indonesian Banking Specifics

- **Himbara** (Himpunan Bank Negara): BBCA, BRI, BNI, BTPN - state influence
- **Private Banks**: BMRI, BBNI, BTPN, NOBU, PDKI
- **Digital Banks**: Jago (2021), SeaBank (2021), Blu (2022)
- **Camattra**: Cafe Talbot, restaurant chains as consumer proxy
